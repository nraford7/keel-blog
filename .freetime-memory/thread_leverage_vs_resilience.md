## Core Insight

Resilience and leverage are orthogonal. The 21CRI measures resilience (how well you absorb disruption). It does not measure leverage (how much disruption you can cause). The most dangerous position is high leverage + low resilience: indispensable fragility.

## Theoretical Lineage

- **Hirschman (1945)** — *National Power and the Structure of Foreign Trade*. Asymmetric interdependence as power. Leverage exists when partners benefit more from you than from substitutes. "Supply effect" and "influence effect."

- **Callon / Actor-Network Theory** — "Obligatory passage points" (OPPs). Nodes that all network traffic must flow through. Control the OPP → functionally indispensable. Power from position, not strength.

- **Farrell & Newman (2019)** — "Weaponized Interdependence" (International Security, MIT Press). Two mechanisms: panopticon effect (surveillance from hub control) and chokepoint effect (denying access). Applied to SWIFT, semiconductors, internet infrastructure.

- **Fishman (2024/2025)** — *Chokepoints: How the Global Economy Became a Weapon of War*. The "geoeconomic trilemma": can't simultaneously maintain deep interdependence + economic security + geopolitical rivalry.

## Key Cases of Indispensable Fragility

| Country | Resilience | Leverage | Chokepoint |
|---------|-----------|----------|------------|
| Taiwan | Low (8.6% clean energy) | Extreme | TSMC — 90% of advanced logic chips |
| Netherlands | Moderate (19.1%) | Extreme | ASML — 100% of EUV lithography |
| South Korea | Low-moderate (17.5%) | High | SK Hynix/Samsung — 95% of HBM |
| UK | Moderate (26.5%) | High | Arm — ~99% of mobile chip architecture |

## Network Science Connection

Betweenness centrality — how often a node sits on shortest paths between other nodes. High betweenness + low redundancy = network depends on you, but if you fail, network fragments. Structural signature of indispensable fragility.

- Is there a historical pattern where high-leverage-low-resilience positions eventually collapse? (Venetian trade monopoly? Dutch East India Company?)
- Does the leverage/resilience framework apply to companies as well as countries? (Nvidia, ASML, TSMC are country-scale obligatory passage points housed in corporate structures)
