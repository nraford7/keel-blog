Art Direction — Keel

Three visual directions. Pick the habitat.

Direction 01

Abyssal Documentary Recommended

MBARI footage × Trevor Paglen × Edward Burtynsky aerials

Real organisms, real landscapes, shot or generated as if by a deep-ocean ROV with clinical lighting that makes alien things look factual. Bioluminescent jellyfish, pheromone-trail macro photography, desire paths shot from directly overhead like satellite imagery. The blog feels like a research station's field journal — precise, reverent, slightly eerie. Every image could be captioned with coordinates.

Mood
Reverent. Scientific wonder without sentimentality.
Lighting
ROV-mounted spots in darkness. The subject glows, the background is abyss.
Color
Desaturated blue-black, teal bioluminescent punctuation, amber instrument readings.
Avoids
Stock nature photography, oversaturated ocean shots, screensaver aesthetics.
Direction 02

Trace Cartography

Edward Tufte × Gerhard Richter × Forensic Architecture

Every image is a map of invisible forces — desire paths rendered as heat maps, ant colonies as network diagrams, Wikipedia edit histories as palimpsests. Layered, translucent, data-as-art. The blog feels like you found a cartographer's private notebook where they mapped things that aren't supposed to have geography.

Mood
Obsessive precision. Beautiful paranoia.
Lighting
Flat, even — like a light table. Objects examined, not dramatized.
Color
Near-monochrome blue-black, precise teal linework, occasional amber data points.
Avoids
Infographics, dashboards, anything resembling a tech company's data viz.
Direction 03

Nocturnal Naturalism

Annie Dillard × Karl Blossfeldt × Edward Weston

The patience of watching one thing for six hours until it reveals itself. Close-up textures of worn earth, grass recovering from footfall, bark beetle galleries, mycelium networks. Intimate, almost uncomfortably close. The blog feels like pressing your face to the ground and discovering the ground is looking back.

Mood
Intimate. Uncomfortably close. Tender.
Lighting
Single source, raking light. Like a flashlight held at ground level at midnight.
Color
Nearly monochrome deep blue-black, teal edge-light where surfaces catch.
Avoids
Nature documentary aesthetics, aerial grandeur, anything pretty. Forensic intimacy, not landscape porn.
