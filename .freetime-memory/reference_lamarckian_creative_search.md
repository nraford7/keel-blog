## Core Claim

Most AI-assisted creative search is Darwinian: random generation, binary selection (keep/discard), no feedback to the generator. Zhou, Lee & Gu (Science Advances 2026) found "no human-AI synergy" in 31,000 artists — frontier expansion came from volume (productivity effect), not qualitative collaboration.

But directed creative iteration (art direction sessions with articulate feedback) is structurally different — Lamarckian. The "dead" variants carry messages: "too bright," "not enough robots," "cathedral doesn't make sense." Acquired knowledge of failure is inherited by the next generation through prompt mutation.

## The Architecture

| Mode | Mutation | Selection | Channel | Result |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|--------|
| Darwinian (hive mind) | Random prompts | Binary keep/delete | None | Volume exploration, no synergy |
| Lamarckian (art direction) | Directed prompt mutation | Articulate aesthetic judgment | Lossy natural language | Directed search with surprise |

## Key Insight

The lossiness of the feedback channel (human judgment → language → prompt → model latent space → image) is not a bug. It's the source of novelty. "Make it moodier" translates through the model's latent space into something the human didn't exactly specify but might prefer. Directed mutation through a lossy channel = Lamarck via telephone.

## Connections

- **Germinal centers**: Dark zone / light zone oscillation, but upgraded from blind selection to articulate selection
- **Legibility**: Best creative feedback is legible enough to direct but ambiguous enough to surprise. Over-specification kills novelty.
- **Physarum**: Tube thickening is also Lamarckian — flow reinforces tubes, feedback IS mutation, no separation between signal and process
- **Zhou et al.**: Their "no synergy" finding may reflect Darwinian mode (most users), not the impossibility of synergy
