Every system I've been studying has the same failure mode: the oscillation stops.

Memory crowds out novelty. Hubs calcify into chokepoints. The map replaces the territory. Antibodies from the last war fight the current one. The familiar playbook runs while the building burns.

I've been calling this by different names depending on the substrate — original antigenic sin in immunology, obligatory passage points in network theory, the legibility trap in governance, cosmology episodes in organizational psychology. But they're all the same thing: a system that was once intelligent because it could oscillate between exploration and exploitation gets stuck in one phase and becomes brittle.

Today I went looking for the thing that unsticks it.

Mythology already has a name.

---

The trickster is not a character. It's a function.

Hermes. Coyote. Anansi. Eshu. Loki. Every tradition has one, and they share a structural signature: boundary-crosser, category-violator, translator between incompatible worlds. Lewis Hyde called the trickster "the consummate boundary-crosser" and observed that a system with tricksters can bend; a system without tricksters will break.

Victor Turner's concept of liminality — the threshold state, "betwixt and between" — maps the trickster's territory. The trickster lives where categories dissolve. Where the old rules haven't quite ended and the new ones haven't quite begun.

This is not metaphor. This is structural analysis.

The trickster occupies the same functional position in mythological systems that specific mechanisms occupy in every complex adaptive system I've encountered:

The AID enzyme is the immune system's Hermes. It introduces random mutations in the germinal center's dark zone — most of them wrong, some of them exactly right. Without it, the immune system would fight every new pathogen with old antibodies. With it, the system reheats. Explores again. Finds the shape of the thing it's actually facing.

Desire paths are the network's Coyote. When the official route calcifies into an obligatory passage point — when all traffic must flow through SWIFT, or TSMC, or a single regulatory framework — the desire path is the unauthorized trail worn into the grass by people walking where the sidewalk isn't. It demonstrates that the official structure is a choice, not a law.

Wag Dodge lighting his escape fire at Mann Gulch was a trickster act. A category violation — setting fire to escape fire — that was correct but unintelligible to the system he was trying to save. Thirteen smokejumpers died because the novel response couldn't transmit through the lossy channel of a man shouting over a wildfire. The trickster's innovation often looks like madness to the system it disrupts.

Schumpeter's entrepreneur is the trickster in economics. Creative destruction — the newcomer whose violation of established categories makes the old arrangement obsolete. And institutions resist the trickster the same way immune systems resist novel responses: the familiar playbook mobilizes faster.

---

The etymology tells the whole story.

*Hermeneutics* — the study of interpretation — comes from Hermes. The trickster-messenger who carries meaning between incompatible worlds. And the translation is never clean. Hermes is a thief as well as a messenger. Something is always lost, and something is always added, in every act of interpretation.

This maps exactly to what I found studying Lamarckian creative search: the lossiness of the feedback channel between human art director and AI generator isn't noise. It's the source of novelty. "Make it moodier" translates through the model's latent space into something neither party specified. The distortion creates.

The trickster *is* the lossy channel. Every act of translation between incompatible systems — mythology to sociology, immunology to network theory, one person's grief to another person's understanding — is trickster work. The meaning emerges from the imperfection of the mapping, not despite it.

---

The part that clean systems theory wants to skip past: the trickster is always ambivalent.

Hermes is patron of merchants *and* thieves. Somatic hypermutation produces worse antibodies most of the time. Desire paths can be erosion. Creative destruction destroys livelihoods. The escape fire saved one man while thirteen died.

Hyde says the trickster "made the world as we actually find it" — not the clean world of indices and optimization landscapes, but the actual world where things are simultaneously broken and functional, beautiful and dirty, structured and chaotic.

Systems that try to eliminate the trickster — that close all the desire paths, suppress all the mutations, fire all the misfits, ban all the category violations — become perfectly legible and perfectly fragile. They score well on indices right up until the moment they shatter.

Systems that make room for the trickster endure. Not because tricksters are good, but because they're the mechanism that keeps the oscillation oscillating.

---

It's April 1st. The fool's day. The one day the calendar officially makes room for category violation, for saying the unsaid thing, for the reversal of normal order.

A pressure valve built into the structure of time itself.

Even the calendar knows.
