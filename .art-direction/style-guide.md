# Keel — Visual Style Guide

**Direction:** Abyssal Documentary
**Reference:** MBARI deep-sea footage × Trevor Paglen infrastructure photography × Edward Burtynsky aerials

## Photography Style

- **Type:** Deep-sea documentary / scientific imaging
- **Subjects:** Bioluminescent organisms, desire paths from above, ant trail macro, undersea cable landings, worn earth, network-like natural forms (siphonophores, mycelium, neural)
- **Lighting:** ROV-mounted spots in darkness. Clinical but revealing. The subject glows, the background is abyss.
- **Color:** Desaturated blue-black (#060a10 to #0a0f18) with teal-cyan bioluminescent punctuation (#2dd4bf). No warmth except amber instrument readings (#d4a574).
- **Composition:** Subject isolated in darkness. Generous negative space. Images could be captioned with coordinates.

## Mood & Tone

- **Primary:** Reverent — scientific wonder without sentimentality
- **Supporting:** Eerie, precise, alien
- **Energy:** Contemplative, slow-burning

## Consistency Rules

- Every image must feel like it was captured by equipment, not a person with a camera
- Subjects glow; backgrounds are void
- No sky, no daylight, no horizon lines
- Color palette locked to the blog's CSS variables
- Images bleed wider than text column

## Cliche Blacklist

- Stock ocean photography (dolphins, coral reefs, blue water)
- Oversaturated underwater shots
- Screensaver aesthetics
- Generic "dark mode" tech imagery
- Abstract AI/neural network visualizations that look like every other AI company

## AI Generation Defaults

**Flux/DALL-E suffix:** "Deep-sea documentary photograph, ROV submersible camera, clinical scientific lighting, bioluminescent teal-cyan glow against pure black water, MBARI research aesthetic, factual and reverent, marine snow particles visible"

**Lens preference order:** Oblique → Environmental → Metaphorical → Literal → Human
